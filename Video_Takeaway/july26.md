## Key Lessons / Takeaways

- AI makes writing code much faster
- But testing, code review, version control, etc. were built for the old, slower speed
- They don't speed up automatically just because code-writing did
- So new problems show up — often in places you didn't expect
- Don't just ask "how do I code faster with AI?"
- Ask: "if output suddenly went up 10x, what breaks first?"

## Key Lessons / Takeaways

- Model tiering: OpenAI's new GPT 5.
Model tiering: OpenAI's new GPT 5.6 comes in three sizes — Sol (biggest/priciest), Terra (middle), and Luna (cheapest) — similar to how Anthropic already splits into Opus/Sonnet/Haiku.
- China is pushing prices down: Chinese labs like Deepseek, Qwen, Kimi, and GLM keep releasing strong models for free/cheap, forcing everyone's prices lower while closing the capability gap with the US.
- Government regulation is the new bottleneck: A US executive order now expects top labs to get government sign-off before releasing very powerful models — Anthropic got burned doing this badly (had to restrict foreign users after release), so OpenAI played it safer by checking in with the government first.
- Risk of "privatized" intelligence: The cheap stuff stays open to everyone, but there's growing worry that the most powerful models might end up reserved for a select few approved companies instead of the public.
- Chips matter as much as the model: Even if a model is smart, real-world speed depends on hardware — OpenAI's Sol model paired with Cerebras chips can hit huge speeds, something China can't easily match yet due to weaker chip infrastructure.
