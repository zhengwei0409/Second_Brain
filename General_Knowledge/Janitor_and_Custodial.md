# Janitor and Custodial

Janitor — a person whose job is to clean and maintain a building (sweeping, mopping, taking out trash, fixing minor things). Common in the US. A more formal/modern term for the same job is custodian.
Custodial — the adjective form related to cleaning/maintenance work or caretaking in general. So:

custodial staff = the cleaning/maintenance team
custodial closet = same as janitor room, just a more formal term
custodian = a janitor (also can mean a legal guardian in other contexts, like "custodial parent" — but that's unrelated to cleaning)

So in a hospital, you might see either "Janitor" or "Custodial" on a door — they basically mean the same thing, just "custodial" sounds a bit more professional/official.

Janitor(清洁工/看门人)

词源来自拉丁语 janua(门),和 January(一月,一年的"门")、Janus(古罗马双面门神)是同一个词根。
记忆画面:一个 janitor 手里拿着一大串钥匙,负责开每一扇门。

Custodial / Custodian(看护人员/监护性质的)

词根是拉丁语 custodia(守护、保护)。
和 custody(监护权,比如 "child custody" 儿童监护权)是同一个词根。
记忆画面:custodian 不只是打扫卫生,更像是这栋楼的"守护者",负责让一切维持良好状态。

快速对比,方便记住:

Janitor → 指"人"(比较口语化、日常用语)
Custodian → 指"人"(比较正式的说法,等于 janitor)
Custodial → 是"形容词",描述和这种看护/清洁工作相关的东西,比如 custodial staff(保洁人员团队)、custodial closet(清洁储物间)、custodial duties(保洁职责)
